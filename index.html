Who Am I

My name is Andrea, and I am a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC) located in New Haven, offering teletherapy to anyone in the state of Connecticut.

I aim to bring empathy, sympathy, compassion, curiosity, authenticity, openness, laughter, and a love of human beings to each of my sessions. I’m on an endless journey of learning, so I’m not afraid to recognize when I’m wrong. I love being exposed to new frameworks and learning from my clients to gain a deeper understanding of their identities.

Learning has been a core theme throughout my life. As a mother of four, I went back to school to get an M.A. in Counseling from Northwestern University and an M.A. in Psychology from Southern Connecticut State University. Before opening my own practice, I worked as a Licensed Clinician and Clinical Supervisor at Shoreline Wellness Center in West Haven and as a Clinician at the Post Traumatic Stress Center in New Haven.

My Approach

  • Core Beliefs

    Nobody is Born Bad

    I believe that nobody is born bad. Instead, we are born with certain tendencies, and the ways we are treated as children form a blueprint for how we live our lives. The problem is that this blueprint that we develop for survival continues with us as we grow older and often stops fitting into the world we know.

    It’s easier to understand why someone might develop PTSD after witnessing a terrible event, but relational/attachment trauma, which develops as a child is growing up, is much more insidious and shapes who that child becomes. Having a loving, attentive caregiver, whether it be a parent, grandparent, aunt/uncle, or family friend, is essential for developing healthy attachment, yet so many of us are not afforded that experience.

    The pain that we experience as children doesn’t magically disappear but is encoded into memory and often left unhealed. Shame is amazing at hiding in the darkness, and it is my job as a counselor to help bring those innermost, hidden pains to the light so that they can be healed. And when one part of the system heals, the whole system can begin healing too.

  • My Approach

    We Are Made up of Many Parts

    My approach is informed by the Internal Family Systems (IFS) model, which suggests that your system is made up of many distinct, and often opposing, parts. At any given moment, each part is struggling for your best interest, trying to keep your system functioning smoothly by minimizing the pain. Although the intentions are good, the repercussions can be deeply painful and even disastrous.

    • Anxiety

    • LGBTQIA+ issues, with a focus on trangender issues
    • Childhood anxiety, including Selective Mutism (SM)

  • Outcomes

    Healing is Possible

    People come to therapy because they’re in pain. Maybe you’re feeling anxious and depressed, you’re having difficulty controlling your anger, you can’t seem to get out of bed, your relationships are suffering, you’re exhausted from constantly trying to change other people or situations, or you feel like there’s a defect in your character that causes others to walk all over you. What underlies every one of these experiences is pain, and together we can change that.

    Through the process of counseling, you can get to a place where your past wounds and hurts are not driving you in everyday life. At your own pace, you can develop trust in all parts of yourself. And when you are able to speak your truth and be witness to the ways that your early experiences shaped who you are today, there can be an unburdening that enables you to heal.

How does teletherapy differ from in-person counseling?

Many of my clients love teletherapy because they don’t have to rely on the unpredictability of public transportation or they otherwise live too far away to commute. Some tell me that they find it easier to open up from the comfort of their own home. However, I realize that teletherapy is not for everyone. If you’re unsure, I encourage you to give it a try. It will feel different at first, but you just might adapt to the pace and rhythm.

How will you relate to my experiences?

I believe in naming things that can be barriers. I am a white, cisgender woman, so I don’t have the same experiences as many of my clients who are people of color, members of the LGBTQ+ community, or belong to other minorities. I never pretend to know something that I don’t, and if I ever get something wrong, please call me out.
